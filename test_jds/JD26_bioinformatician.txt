
Job title: Bioinformatician

Role Summary:
We are seeking a skilled and motivated Bioinformatician to join our research team. This hybrid role combines expertise in biology, computer science, and statistics to analyze and interpret large-scale biological datasets. You will be responsible for developing and applying computational tools and pipelines for genomic, transcriptomic, proteomic, or other 'omics' data analysis, contributing to cutting-edge research projects and discoveries.
Key Responsibilities:
Analyze and interpret complex biological data, including Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS) data (e.g., RNA-Seq, DNA-Seq, ChIP-Seq), microarray data, and proteomics data.
Develop, implement, and maintain bioinformatics pipelines and workflows for data processing, quality control, and analysis.
Utilize and adapt existing bioinformatics tools and databases, and develop custom scripts and software as needed.
Apply statistical methods for data analysis, including differential gene expression, variant calling, pathway analysis, and network analysis.
Collaborate closely with wet-lab biologists and researchers to understand experimental design, interpret results, and formulate new hypotheses.
Manage and curate large biological datasets, ensuring data integrity and accessibility.
Prepare clear and concise reports, visualizations, and presentations of analytical results for internal meetings, publications, and grant proposals.
Stay current with the latest advancements in bioinformatics, computational biology, and relevant biological fields.
Contribute to the writing of scientific manuscripts and grant applications.
Provide bioinformatics support and training to other researchers.
Required Qualifications:
Master's or PhD degree in Bioinformatics, Computational Biology, Genomics, Computer Science, Statistics, or a related field with a strong bioinformatics component.
2-3+ years of post-Master's or relevant PhD experience in bioinformatics analysis.
Proficiency in programming languages commonly used in bioinformatics (e.g., Python, R, Perl).
Strong experience with Linux/Unix operating systems and shell scripting.
Demonstrated experience in analyzing large-scale biological datasets (e.g., NGS data).
Familiarity with common bioinformatics tools, databases (e.g., NCBI, Ensembl, UCSC Genome Browser), and algorithms.
Solid understanding of molecular biology, genetics, and genomics principles.
Strong statistical analysis skills.
Excellent problem-solving, analytical, and critical thinking abilities.
Good communication and collaboration skills, with the ability to work effectively in an interdisciplinary team.
Preferred Qualifications:
Experience with cloud computing environments (e.g., AWS, Azure, GCP) for bioinformatics analysis.
Experience with machine learning techniques applied to biological data.
Knowledge of specific 'omics' data analysis (e.g., single-cell RNA-seq, metagenomics).
Track record of scientific publications in peer-reviewed journals.
Experience with workflow management systems (e.g., Nextflow, Snakemake).
