Job title: Pediatrician

Summary

The American Medical Center was founded in 2012 to address and help fill the needs of the Americans stationed in the Kaiserslautern Military Community. Reportedly, the lack of "available space" of medical care on base and the headaches created by the “pay-and-claim” medical care of the German economy caused frustrations with many stationed Americans.

As demand for medical services grew, the American Medical Center has expanded its on-site medical services to now include more family practice physicians, physical therapists, psychiatrists, nutritional counseling, and veterans’ evaluation services. Today the American Medical Center provides many services across two locations, and we are looking for a Pediatrician to join our team!

Qualifications

Medical degree from an accredited medical school, this includes PAs, NPs and other qualified providers
Current Basic Life Support (BLS) or cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) certification
Valid medical license to practice as a Pediatrician or Family Practice Doctor;a highly desired applicant will hold multiple state licenses
Excellent clinical skills and knowledge of pediatric medicine or family medicine with emphasis on holistic and natural remedies
Strong communication and interpersonal skills to effectively interact with patients, parents, and healthcare team members
Ability to provide compassionate care to children of all ages and able to listen to parent's concerns
Proficiency in electronic medical record systems
Read, understand, speak, and write English fluently

Responsibilities

Diagnose and treat acute and chronic illnesses in children
Order and interpret diagnostic tests, such as blood work or imaging studies
Prescribe medications and develop treatment plans; encouraging natural remedies when possible and using antibiotic stewardship for the best possible outcome for the patient
Provide counseling on preventive healthcare measures, including family planning and nutritional therapy
Collaborate with other healthcare professionals to coordinate patient care
